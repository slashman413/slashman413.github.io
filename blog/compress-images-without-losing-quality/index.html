Lossy vs lossless compression

  • Lossy (JPG, WebP) throws away detail the eye barely notices to achieve much smaller files. At sensible quality settings (around 75–85%) the result looks identical to most viewers.
  • Lossless (PNG, lossless WebP) keeps every pixel and shrinks files by packing data more efficiently — smaller savings, but perfect fidelity. Best for logos, screenshots and graphics with sharp edges.

How to compress without visible quality loss

  1. Resize first. The biggest win is dimensions: a 6000px photo displayed at 1200px is wasting ~96% of its pixels. Scale to the size you'll actually show.
  2. Pick the right format (WebP for web, JPG for photos, PNG for graphics).
  3. Tune the quality slider to about 75–85% for lossy formats — compare before/after and stop when you can't see a difference.
  4. Strip metadata (EXIF/location) to save a little more and protect privacy.

Why compress in your browser?

Many "free" compressors upload your images to a server. For private photos, ID documents or client work, that's a real concern. A browser-based tool processes the image locally on your device, so nothing ever leaves your computer — faster and far more private.

FAQ

Will compression ruin my image? Not at sensible settings. Lossy at 80% quality is visually identical for most photos; lossless formats keep every pixel.

How small can I make a file? Resizing plus WebP often cuts 70–90% off a typical phone photo with no visible difference.
